The Problem We Face

Our local rivers, the Mula and Mutha, are choking on pollution. Untreated sewage, industrial discharge, and widespread plastic waste threaten our city's water supply and precious ecosystems.

But it's not too late. Project River Revive is a community-driven initiative for Punekars, by Punekars, dedicated to the hands-on cleaning and long-term preservation of our rivers.

Our Mission

To mobilize our community through direct action, education, and advocacy to achieve a clean, healthy, and sustainable river ecosystem for generations to come.

Clean Up

Organize and execute regular, large-scale river bank cleanup events.

Educate

Raise public awareness about the sources of river pollution and its impact.

Restore

Work with local authorities on long-term restoration and re-greening projects.

Understanding the Crisis in Pune

The Causes

  • Plastic & Solid Waste: Dumping of garbage directly into the river.
  • Untreated Sewage: A high percentage of the city's sewage enters the rivers untreated.
  • Construction Debris: Illegal dumping from rapid urban development.
  • Industrial Effluents: Release of chemicals from nearby industrial areas.

The Consequences

  • Water-borne Diseases: Health crisis for communities relying on river water.
  • Loss of Biodiversity: Fish and native plant life are dying.
  • Groundwater Pollution: Contaminants are seeping into our groundwater.
  • Flooding: Blockages from waste increase flood risk during monsoon.

What You Can Do

  • Reduce Plastic: Carry reusable bags, bottles, and tiffins.
  • Proper Waste Segregation: Ensure wet and dry waste is managed correctly.
  • Stop Dumping: Educate others and report illegal dumping.
  • Join Us: Volunteer for a cleanup drive!
